What Is It

Formaldehyde (CH2O) is a colorless gas at room temperature. It has a strong, pungent odor.

Formaldehyde is a natural chemical found in the environment. Humans and most other living organisms make small amounts as part of normal metabolic processes.

Formaldehyde is used in manufactured wood products, such as:

  • particleboard
  • plywood
  • fiberboard
  • glues and adhesives
  • permanent-press fabrics
  • paper product coatings
  • insulation materials

Within a few hours, formaldehyde breaks down in the air. It is called formalin when dissolved in water, which is commonly used as an industrial disinfectant and as a medical preservative. It is also used to make other chemicals, such as food preservatives, antiseptics, medicines, and cosmetics.

Sometimes, although formaldehyde is not used, substances that release formaldehyde are used in manufacturing. These substances are used in cosmetics, soaps, shampoos, lotions and sunscreens, and cleaning products.

Formaldehyde can be added as a preservative to food, but it can also be produced as the result of cooking and smoking.
